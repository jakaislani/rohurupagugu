Career

Early Days

Hannibal did comedy for the first time in 2002 while he was attending college.

He made his TV debut in 2007 in “The Late Late Show with Craig Ferguson” and also released his first stand-up album, “My Name is Hannibal,” in July 2010.

From 2009 to 2010, he wrote for “Saturday Night Live” and made an appearance on the show twice, in the Megan Fox/U2 episode and the Charles Barkley/Alicia Keys episode.

“Variety” magazine named Buress one of “Ten Comics to Watch in 2010,” and in September of that year, he joined the writing staff of NBC’s “30 Rock.”

Buress quit the writing job after six months, but he appeared on the show nine times between 2010 and 2012.

His second album, 2012’s “Animal Furnace,” also aired as a Comedy Central special, and the network aired the special “Hannibal Buress Live from Chicago” in 2014.

In 2016, Netflix released “Hannibal Buress: Comedy Camisado” and the documentary “Hannibal Takes Edinburgh,” and in 2020, he released his special “Miami Nights” on YouTube.

Similarly, Hannibal has appeared as himself on the television series “Louie,” “The Jim Gaffigan Show,” “Children’s Hospital,” and  “Crashing,” and the films “Sleepwalk with Me” and “The Comedian.”

Career Peak

Hannibal Buress played Officer Watkins in “Neighbors” and 2016’s “Neighbors 2: Sorority Rising” and Coach Wilson in the blockbuster film “Spider-Man: Homecoming,” which grossed $880.2 million at the box office.

Likewise, He has appeared in the films “The Kings of Summer,” “Daddy’s Home,” “The Nice Guys,” “The Disaster Artist,” “Baywatch,” “Blockers,” and “Slice.”

Moreover, He voices Buddy, the dachshund in the “The Secret Life of Pets” franchise, which has grossed more than $1.3 billion worldwide.

Furthermore, Hannibal has guest-starred on “Kroll Show,” “The Mindy Project,” and “High Maintenance” and done voice acting on “Bob’s Burgers,” “China, IL,” “BoJack Horseman,” and “The Simpsons.”

Also, He played Lincoln Rice on Comedy Central’s “Broad City,” appearing in 28 episodes from 2014 to 2019, and he co-hosted 45 episodes of “The Eric Andre Show” before leaving in 2020.

Additionally, Buress has also appeared in the web series “Hot Ones” (2018) and “The Bob Ross Challenge” (2020), and he can be heard in the video games “Grand Theft Auto V” (2013) and “NBA 2K17” (2016).
